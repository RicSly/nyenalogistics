Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our shipping services

What documents are required for international shipping?

Typically required documents include: Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Bill of Lading/Air Waybill, Certificate of Origin, Export/Import Licenses (if applicable), Insurance Certificate, and any country-specific documentation. Our team will guide you through the exact requirements based on your cargo and destination.

How long does sea freight take from Accra to Lagos?

Standard sea freight from Accra to Lagos typically takes 7-10 days, including port handling and customs clearance. This can vary based on weather conditions, port congestion, and specific shipping schedules. Express options may be available for faster delivery.

Do you handle customs clearance?

Yes, we provide complete customs clearance services for both export and import shipments. Our team of customs experts handles all documentation, duty calculations, and clearance procedures to ensure smooth transit through customs checkpoints.

What types of cargo do you not accept?

We do not accept illegal substances, weapons, ammunition, live animals without proper documentation, perishable items without proper packaging/refrigeration, and any items prohibited by international law or carrier restrictions. For hazardous materials, special arrangements and documentation are required.

How is shipping cost calculated?

Shipping costs are calculated based on: Cargo weight and dimensions (chargeable weight), Origin and destination, Shipping method (air/sea/land), Type of cargo and special handling requirements, Insurance value, Customs duties and taxes, and Additional services required (packaging, storage, etc.).

Can I track my shipment in real-time?

Yes, all shipments come with real-time tracking. You'll receive a tracking number and can monitor your cargo's progress through our online portal or mobile app. We provide regular updates at key milestones throughout the shipping journey.
